My intended outcomes for this course are clearly split into two parts. The long term ambition for me is that I would like to make games, either for a small studio or just for myself. I'm not terribly motivated by money (partly due to the privilege of not ever wanting for it in my life, partly due to complete disillusionment in capitalism), so my short term goal is to get a job doing something that affords me the flexibility to work while still pursuing music, which is my original passion. In some ways it feels dishonest doing this course, as I have no real ambition or desire to be a web developer. This course is definitely a stepping stone for me, but I know my reasons for going this route and I stick by them.

My biggest weakness in all of this will be, I think, my lack of effective time management. A lot of the pitfalls that have already been laid out in this course are things I frequently suffer from; particularly getting sucked down rabbit holes, but also more generic issues with procrastination. This means that I don't necessarily handle being left responsible for my own learning to this degree. However, one thing I am very confident in is my ability to stick to commitments that I make to others. I will not allow myself to let other people down, and that includes not only my peers who may be counting on me for a team project, but also the facilitators and teachers who are putting trust in me to follow through. These are things I take increidbly seriously, and that will be what guides my decision making in all this.

Before this course began I made a speculative daily calendar to block out my time effectively, but this was built on some guesses about the workload that turned out to be inaccurate, and so I kind of abandoned it. However this week has shown me that it's definitely still something that I need guiding me, and so I will be revisiting and adjusting that to serve as a template for my time spent studying at home. Once Bootcamp starts I am already committed to spending as much time as I can at the facilities, as my past experience has shown me I learn best by creating a physically distinct space free of distractions, and my living situation simply doesn't allow that at present. I have set aside the full time of the course to be as free of any other schedule conflicts, although there is always the slight chance of a gig opportunity I have to take (but even that is usually something that can fit around pretty easily); this is pretty unlikely at the moment though (lol).

I'm very lucky to have an incredible support base of friends, family and my partner to call on when I start feeling overwhelmed. I also have a good friend who did the course last year, and who has been an incredible help in getting me this far. I also know a couple of the facilitators, and the ones I've met since do the course I also feel very comfortable talking to. This is important because one of the things I know I'm also not great with (and that I want to improve as well) is making friends with people. I'm not necessarily uncomfortable talking to new people or being friendly or open with them, but I think I need specific conditions for that to happen naturally and being in a group chat doesn't really meet those conditions for me. I've really had to acknowledge this in the past couple of weeks as I know I haven't been participating a great deal in the Slack comms, but it's something I want to work on.

So I've got my work cut out for me, but don't we all? I think if I can come out of this course with an improved sense of discipline and scheduling ability, as well as the programming skills to continue and build on a potential career in coding somewhere (hopefully gaming but maybe music tech or something else), that will be a real success. And I think I can do that.
